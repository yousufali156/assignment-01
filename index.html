The Vision of Future

The vision of a New Bangladesh focuses on innovation through digital transformation, sustainable development, and inclusive growth. Emphasizing technology and education, the nation aims to become a global economic powerhouse while addressing challenges like infrastructure, climate change, and inequality. The future of Bangladesh envisions a resilient, thriving society driven by creativity, collaboration, and progress.

Digital Transformation

Green Energy

Green energy refers to energy derived from renewable, environmentally friendly sources like solar, wind, and hydropower, reducing carbon emissions.

Modern Infrastructure

Modern infrastructure refers to advanced systems and structures such as transportation, utilities. designed to support sustainable growth and improve quality of life.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi economist, social entrepreneur, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate. He founded the Grameen Bank in 1983, pioneering the concept of microfinance to provide small, collateral-free loans to poor people, especially women. This initiative has empowered millions to escape poverty and become self-reliant. Currently, he serves as the Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh, leading the interim government during a transitional period. He received the 2006 Nobel Peace Prize for his contributions to economic and social development. Dr. Yunus is also a strong advocate for social business, focusing on solving societal issues over profit. Despite challenges in Bangladesh, he remains a global figure promoting poverty alleviation and sustainable development.

Hilsha fish (known locally as Ilish)

Hilsha fish (Ilish) is the national fish of Bangladesh, highly valued for its rich flavor and cultural importance. It is commonly enjoyed during festivals and is a key part of traditional Bengali cuisine.
